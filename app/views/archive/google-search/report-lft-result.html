Use this service to report your result after you've used a rapid lateral flow self-test kit for COVID-19.

A rapid lateral flow test is a COVID-19 test you can do yourself. If shows you the result on a handheld device that comes with the test.

Reporting a rapid lateral flow test result is different to registering a test kit. You need to register your test kit if the test results will be sent to you. Find out how to register your test kit on NHS.UK.

Report the result:

  • every time you use a rapid lateral flow test kit, even if the result is negative
  • as soon as possible after you get the result

You cannot report a result after more than 24 hours.

You can only report one result at a time.

Start now

Before you start

To use this service, you need:

  • the barcode on the test strip or the ID number printed under it (the test strip is the part of the kit that shows your result)
  • a mobile phone number so we can text you to confirm we’ve got your result

Report by phone

You can also report your test result by calling the phone number in your test kit’s instructions.

If you have a problem with your test kit

Call 119 or report the problem online.

Why you should report your results

Reporting results helps to:

  • reduce infection rates in your community
  • protect people at higher risk of infection
  • prevent and reduce the spread of the virus
  • find out if you’re infectious (you may not have symptoms)

Self-isolation

You and your household may need to self-isolate depending on your result.

Get advice about how to self-isolate.

{{ pageTitle }}

Report your result every time you test yourself to see if you’re currently infectious using a coronavirus (COVID-19) self-test kit (also called a rapid or lateral flow test kit).

Self-testing and reporting the result helps:

  • reduce infection rates in your community
  • protect people at higher risk of infection
  • prevent and reduce the spread of the virus
  • detect positive cases (you may not have symptoms)

Find out more about self-testing.

Use this service to

  • report your result
  • find out what your result means
  • get advice on what you must do if your result is positive, negative or invalid
! Warning You may need to self-isolate depending on your result. The NHS has information to help you and your family self-isolate.

What you need to do

Follow the instructions that come with your kit.

Report your result when you get it.

Do this every time you self-test. Report your result no matter what it is.

Before you start

To report your result, you need the barcode on the test strip or the ID number printed under it.

You need to give your contact details.

You’re also asked things like your age and gender. This is only used for research purposes.

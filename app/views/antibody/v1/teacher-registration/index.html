You can now sign up for a fingerprick blood test (antibody test) if you're a teacher or teaching support staff.

It does not matter if you've had coronavirus before or not.

About the test

The coronavirus (COVID-19) antibody test is a fingerprick blood test that you do at home (it is sent to you in the post).

The home test kit comes with everything you need and simple step-by-step instructions. There's a video to help you.

You take the test and return your sample in the post. You'll get your results a few days later.

Why take the test

The test checks for coronavirus antibodies. If you have them, it's likely you've had the virus before.

Your details will help the NHS and research scientists. They need to learn more about who has already had the virus and how it is spreading.

This is not an immunity test. It cannot tell you when you had the virus or how effective (if at all) any antibodies are. COVID-19 is a new virus and doctors do not yet know if it is possible for you to catch it again or spread it.

Learn more about what antibody testing will tell you

How to sign up

You'll need a mobile phone that can receive text messages.

You'll be asked some questions to check you're eligible and to get some basic information about you. It takes 5 to 10 minutes to sign up.

Then, you'll need to wait to receive a text. The text will invite you to order a home test kit when one becomes available.

! Warning Most people will receive their invite within a few weeks, but it can take up to 3 months.

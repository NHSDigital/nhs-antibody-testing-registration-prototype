We can only give tests to people with symptoms and people who have been asked to get tested. That's because there's a limited number of tests right now.

Also, you may not be able to get a test if your symptoms started too long ago.

Get advice on what to do next

If one of these situations applies to you, there are steps you can take to look after yourself and others.

I'm a contact of someone who has tested positive for coronavirus, but I do not have symptoms

You need to self-isolate for 14 days from when you were in contact with that person.

Read more advice about when to self-isolate and what to do.

You do not need a coronavirus test unless you go on to have symptoms.

I'm planning to visit someone at higher risk from coronavirus and want to make sure I do not have the virus

Avoid close contact with this person.

Wash your hands regularly, use a face covering when social distancing is not possible, and try to keep your distance from them.

Find out more about social distancing: what you need to do.

You can also read advice for people at high risk from coronavirus.

My employer or school has told me to get a test even though I do not have symptoms

If your employer or school has asked you to get tested, and you do not have symptoms, you can pay for a private test.

In this case, you cannot use this free service to get a test.

I need to get a test to travel to another country

If your travel company has asked you to get tested, and you do not have symptoms, you can pay for a private test.

In this case, you cannot use this free service to get a test.

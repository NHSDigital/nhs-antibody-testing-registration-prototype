What to do when you receive your test kit

  1. Read the instructions in your test kit carefully
  2. Register your kit online so we know who to send the results to. The registration link is https://gov.uk/register-home-test.
  3. Take the test. Here is a helpful video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zCqo7MhQT6U
  4. Follow the instructions on how to return the kit. It's very important you do this as close to when you take the test as possible.

How different ages should do the test

  • 18+ years old - do the test on yourself (unless unable to do so)
  • 12 to 17 years old - do the test on themselves with adult supervision
  • 11 years and under - an adult should do the test on the child

Testing is available for children of all ages.

If you're worried

If at any stage over the next few days, you or someone you live with gets worse, or your symptoms do not get better after 7 days, use the online 111 coronavirus service https://111.nhs.uk/COVID-19. Only call 111 if you cannot get help online. In a medical emergency call 999.

Call 111 if you're worried about a baby or child. Call 999 if the baby or child seems very unwell, is getting worse, or you think there's something seriously wrong.

Getting the results

We'll send your test results to you by text and email using the contact details you've provided.

If you test positive for coronavirus, we'll get in touch to find out who you may have come into contact with. We'll then contact them confidentially to tell them to self-isolate. This is called contact tracing and will help to protect you and others.

Getting help

If you have any problems, call the helpline:

  • England, Wales and Northern Ireland - call 119 (free from mobiles and landlines)
  • Scotland - call 0300 303 2713 (charged at your standard network rate)

The helpline is open from 7am to 11pm.

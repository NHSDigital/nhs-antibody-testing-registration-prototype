In England, you can order tests if:

  • you have a health condition which means you're eligible for new COVID-19 treatments (opens in new tab)
  • you've been told to get a test because you're going into hospital for a procedure
  • your GP or healthcare professional has asked you to get a test

You can also order tests if:

  • you work for the NHS in a patient-facing role
  • you work for an NHS-commissioned independent healthcare provider in a patient-facing role looking after NHS patients
  • you work for the NHS and you have one of the 3 main COVID-19 symptoms (opens in new tab) or you have recently tested positive and need to take a test to return to work
  • you work in the adult social care sector as a personal assistant, a Shared Lives carer, a Care Quality Commission (CQC) inspector or a social worker (opens in new tab)

If you are not eligible for tests

Do not call 119, as you will not be able to get a test this way. You cannot order tests from NHS 111 or 999.

Find out what to do if you think you have COVID-19. If you're worried, or your symptoms get worse, contact 111 or speak to your GP. In an emergency dial 999.

If you do not have symptoms, find out how to avoid catching and spreading COVID-19. You can also find advice and support if you're worried about COVID-19.

If they test positive for COVID-19, and they agree to antibody testing, we may send them antibody tests.

Antibody tests help the NHS learn how likely people are to get COVID-19 a second time or after a vaccination, and the impact of variants.

Not everyone who tests positive for COVID-19 will be sent antibody tests because there is a limited number. If they are sent antibody tests, they will:

  • get a text telling them an antibody test is on its way
  • be posted 2 antibody tests 3 weeks apart to their home address
  • do a fingerprick blood test on themselves at home each time
  • need to post back the completed antibody tests
  • get the test results sent to them

About twice-weekly testing for coronavirus (COVID-19)

Dear {{ data['first-name'] or 'John' }} {{ data['last-name'] or 'Smith' }}

Thank you for taking part in regular twice weekly testing.

Your test pack should have arrived. If not, use the tracking email sent to you by Royal Mail, or call the contact centre (details below).

1). Why regular testing if you do not have symptoms is important

Around 1 in 3 people with coronavirus do not have any symptoms. This means they could be spreading coronavirus without knowing it.

Testing twice a week (3 or 4 days apart) and reporting every result to the NHS on time will:

  • stop the spread of the virus
  • reduce transmission/infection rates
  • help protect the NHS and save lives

2). Your household (or bubble) should test twice a week

It's best if your household (or bubble) all test twice a week 3 or 4 days apart. Try to do this all on the same day, and around the same time.

Avoid an invalid (void) result:

  • always read the instructions before you start
  • watch the video on how to take a rapid test
  • get the instructions in other languages and formats (easy read / large print)

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/rapid-lateral-flow tests

3). Report every result

Report every result (whatever it is) to the NHS on the day you take the test (and no later than 24 hours later)

To save time, report online and use your NHS Login (you can set one up the first time you report).

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/report-covid19-result

4). Order more tests

Your test pack contains 7 tests.

You can quickly and easily get more. Do this before you take your last test.

There are 2 ways:

  • collect from a test site - get it the same day
  • order online - get it within 2 days

Go to GOV.UK: www.gov.uk/rapid-lateral-flow tests

Order for your own household (not for the bubble).

5). Stop twice weekly testing if...

If anyone in the household (or bubble) tests positive or gets coronavirus symptoms, tell the school and:

  • stop using the tests
  • you must all stay at home
  • those with symptoms must self-isolate immediately
  • those with symptoms must get a different test to check if they have the virus
  • follow the advice given to you when they get their results

Get NHS help and advice: www.nhs.uk/coronavirus

6). The contact centre is here to help

If you have any questions or problems call the contact centre.

Call 119 in England, Wales and Northern Ireland
Free from mobiles and landlines

Call 0300 303 2713 in Scotland
Charged at your standard network rate

Lines open 7am to 11pm
